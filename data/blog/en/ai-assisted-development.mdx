## Three Levels of AI Usage

Most engineers I observe tend to use AI in roughly three levels:

1. **Q&A level**: treating AI like an interactive Stack Overflow
2. **Assist level**: using AI for autocomplete, explanations, or simple refactors
3. **Workflow level**: integrating AI into design, implementation planning, review, and documentation

Most teams stay in the first two levels.

I have been trying to push further into the third.

## What I Actually Use

### GitHub Copilot

I mainly use Copilot during day-to-day coding. The biggest value is not just autocomplete. It is speeding up the parts I already understand but do not want to rewrite by hand over and over.

### Claude Code / Codex CLI

I use these tools for tasks that require more context and structure:

- understanding an unfamiliar codebase
- generating a first draft from specs or conventions
- preparing the first pass of a broader refactor

### Project instructions and skills

This is the part I find most structurally valuable.

When a project has clear instructions, skills, conventions, and publishing rules, AI agents can build context much faster and produce much more useful output.

That is why I spend time on things like:

- repository-level instructions
- project architecture notes
- publishing workflows
- prompts and skill files that explain how the codebase is organized

### MCP

I still think MCP is early, but it is one of the most interesting pieces of infrastructure in this space.

It moves AI beyond code reading into something more connected to the real system: documentation, tools, and operational context.

## Principles I Keep Coming Back To

**AI does not replace architectural judgment.** It is good at producing a first draft, but engineers still need to decide what is safe, maintainable, and worth keeping.

**Context quality shapes output quality.** Better project instructions usually matter more than clever one-off prompts.

**AI works best as a collaborator.** I define the problem, AI helps explore or draft, and I make the final engineering decision.
