## Before We Start

After all these years as a software developer, you've definitely Googled other people's blogs to track down solutions to coding problems — reading how someone else filled the holes they fell into.

As a developer, I can't contribute the way the greats do: those crystal-clear deep dives, battle-hardened war stories, and articles that pull you in from the first line.

But I have been doing frontend development for over 10 years. I've built plenty of components, handled performance issues, spent time in the e-commerce space that most people interact with every day, and participated in building a logistics system from scratch.

The thing is — now that AI can fill the gaps in my productivity, help me review my work, and make side projects so much easier to spin up, it honestly feels great. So let's give this a try. Consider it me pushing myself to keep shipping.

## What This Blog Is For

Beyond being a place where people can learn about my background, I want this site to contribute something — sharing my own perspective and recording what I know and learn along the way.

I want it to do at least three things:

1. **Help people quickly understand who I am**: my background, technical direction, and the kinds of tech topics I follow
2. **Show how I think about frontend and system design**: working through architecture decisions, trade-offs, and the painful parts — and honestly, writing things down helps me remember them better too
3. **A base for ongoing output**: technical notes, system lessons, and AI-assisted development experiments

## What I Plan to Write About

The main themes are:

- **Frontend Architecture**: Next.js, React, component design, data flow, and performance
- **Enterprise Systems**: the architectures I designed for e-commerce platforms and logistics freight-forwarding systems, and how I solved the problems along the way
- **AI-assisted Development**: my adventure exploring AI-assisted tooling
- **Notes / TIL**: short notes on things everyone else already knew — but I didn't, so I'm writing them down

## Finally

The posts here will not be step-by-step tutorial walkthroughs.

What I want to write more about is: **why I made this choice, what the trade-offs were, where the system starts to hurt, and how to keep it running in a real team over time**.
