## Before We Start

I've been working in frontend for more than 10 years. I've built a lot of components, handled my share of performance issues, and gone through multiple large-system refactors across e-commerce and logistics products.

What I haven't done enough of is writing those lessons down.

This blog is my correction.

## What This Blog Is For

I want this site to do at least three things well:

1. **Help people quickly understand who I am**: my background, technical direction, and the kinds of problems I care about
2. **Show how I think about frontend and system design**: not just code snippets, but architecture decisions, trade-offs, and the painful parts that show up later
3. **Give me a real publishing base**: a place for technical notes, system lessons, and AI-assisted development experiments

## What I Plan to Write About

The main themes are:

- **Frontend Architecture**: Next.js, React, component boundaries, data flow, and maintainability
- **Enterprise Systems**: frontend challenges inside B2B systems, logistics products, and e-commerce platforms
- **AI-assisted Development**: how I use Copilot, Codex, Claude, MCP, and instructions as part of engineering workflows
- **Notes / TIL**: short records of what I tested, what broke, and what I learned

These topics come directly from the work I have done.

From large-scale e-commerce at Newegg to more recent logistics and forwarding systems, I have become more convinced that useful writing is not only about "how to do it." It is about **why the decision made sense, where it breaks, and how a team can keep it maintainable over time**.

## One More Note

The posts here will usually not be step-by-step tutorials.

What I care more about is: **why I made this choice, what the trade-offs were, where the system starts to hurt, and what helps a team keep moving**.

If you work on similar problems, I hope this site is useful.
